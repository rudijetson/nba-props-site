How the Projection Works

PROJ = Base Rate √ó Minutes √ó Opp √ó Home √ó Rest
1. Base Rate (per-minute production)

Weighted average: 60% last 10 games + 40% season. Recent form matters more than early-season games.

2. Minutes (expected playing time)

Same weighting: 60% L10 + 40% season. Captures recent rotation changes.

3. Opponent Factor (defensive matchup)

How many points/rebounds/assists does the opponent allow vs league average?
OPP > 1.0 = bad defense (boost projection)
OPP < 1.0 = good defense (lower projection)

4. Location Factor (home/away split)

Player's per-minute production at home (or away) vs their overall per-minute rate.
Uses shrinkage toward 1.0 based on sample size (full effect at 15+ games), capped ¬±15%.

5. Rest Factor (fatigue adjustment)

Back-to-back = 0.95 (5% reduction)
Normal rest = 1.0
Extra rest (3+ days) = 1.02 (2% boost)

Edge Calculation

We convert the projection to a probability using the normal distribution (accounting for game-to-game variance), then compare to the book's implied probability after removing vig.

Edge = Our Proj% ‚àí Book's Fair%

A 15% edge means we think the bet hits 15 percentage points more often than the book does.
